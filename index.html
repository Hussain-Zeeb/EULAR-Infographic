Recognising Still’s Disease as a single condition

A Unified Perspective on Still’s Disease

Systemic juvenile idiopathic arthritis (sJIA) and adult-onset Still’s disease (AOSD) should be recognised as a single condition, Still’s disease, and not seen as separate disorders.1 This is the key recommendation from the 2024 EULAR/PReS consensus report on the diagnosis and management of Still’s disease. 

Understanding that Still’s disease is a single disease entity is key to addressing the shared symptoms, diagnostic challenges, and treatment strategies for patients of all ages. Explore how these guidelines can help to standardise care and may enhance outcomes for everyone living with Still’s disease.1
